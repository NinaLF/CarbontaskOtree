Carbon footprints are typically measured in CO2 equivalents, allowing to include other greenhouse gases additional to CO2 (e.g., methane).
This is not something people are very familar with, so we just ask you to make your best estimate without looking up any information. We are interested in your perceptions.

We will ask you to give your best estimation of the size of the personal carbon footprint of a series of people with different lifestyles.
Since the international unit for measuring this is 'metric tons and kilos', we will ask you to make your estimates in kg of CO2eq .
1 metric ton CO2 equals 1000 kg CO2. And 1000 kg equal 2205 pounds.

In the United Kingdom each person has on average a total carbon footprint of about 7200 kgs. This is an average and can differ widely between people based on their income and lifestyle.
To give you a better idea of the unit CO2 emissions: An average car emits about 1'000 kg CO2 (2'205 pounds) for about every 2'500 miles driven.

Please note that the individual carbon footprints you will assess are simplified, based on only 6 behaviors, so they do not account for a person's entire carbon footprint.
Next you will see how the task will look like.
Then we ask you to estimate the carbon footprints of 16 persons with different lifestyles .
