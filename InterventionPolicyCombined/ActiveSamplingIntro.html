A personal carbon footprint refers to the total amount of greenhouse gases (including CO2 and methane) emitted into the atmosphere as a result of an individual's behavior.
The carbon footprint thus includes emissions from driving, flying, using electricity, cooling or heating your home, purchasing goods and other sources.
In other words, a personal carbon footprint reflects an individual's total impact on the climate system.

Why does this matter?
To meet the Paris Agreement goals (limiting global warming to 1.5–2°C), the UK must cut its annual emissions roughly in half by 2035.
This will require both policy changes and behavior changes.

On the next pages you will be able to see and explore the emissions different behaviors and behavior changes cause.
