A personal carbon footprint refers to the total amount of greenhouse gases (including CO2 and methane) that are emitted into the atmosphere as a result of an individual,s behavior.
The carbon footprint thus includes emissions from driving, flying, using electricity, cooling or heating your home, purchasing goods and other sources.
In other words, a personal carbon footprint reflects an individual's total impact on the climate system.

A carbon tax puts a price on greenhouse gas emissions. It applies to activities that release emissions, such as using electricity, driving, flying, or producing food.
The higher the emissions associated with a product or service, the higher the cost due to the tax. By making high-emission goods like gasoline, fuel for heating, or beef more expensive, the tax encourages cleaner alternatives and helps reduce emissions.

Why does this matter?
To meet the Paris Agreement goals (limiting global warming to 1.5–2°C), the UK must cut its annual emissions roughly in half by 2035. This yearly reduction equals about 191 million tons of CO2 .
This will require both policy changes and behavior changes.
